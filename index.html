I

The Chronicle

Who wanders here

— sealed likeness of the archivist

Long ago I traded the warm south for the high, hushed places where design breathes slower. I build for the web — but I think like a smith. Every button should feel forged. Every transition should land like snow, not slam like a door.

My work lives at the meeting of engineering and enchantment: performant front-ends, motion that means something, and interfaces that keep their composure under storm. I care about the cold details most people never notice — until they do.

  • Restraint — every effect earns its place.
  • Craft — legible at a glance, deep on inspection.
  • Wonder — software should still feel like magic.
