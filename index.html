I

The Great Hall

You are inside. The fires are lit.

— the keeper, by firelight

Outside, everything is stone and snow. In here I keep the work: interfaces forged for the web, built the way a smith builds — slowly, deliberately, to outlast the weather. Every button struck true. Every transition landing like snow, never slamming like a door.

My craft lives where engineering meets ritual: performant front-ends, motion that means something, and systems that keep their composure under storm. The cold details most people never notice — until they do.

  • Restraint — every effect earns its place.
  • Craft — legible at a glance, deep on inspection.
  • Wonder — software should still feel like magic.
